Localized Data for Policy Evaluation

Public officials in NYC currently lack access to localized data to evaluate the effectiveness of air quality regulations on a street-by-street basis. Our dashboard bridges this gap by offering real-time, hyper-local air pollution data, enabling more accurate assessments of policies and their impact on air quality throughout the city.

The Solution

Crowdsourced Data Collection

Cyclists contribute to a richer data network by:

  • Purchasing or making DIY affordable sensors to monitor air quality in real-time.
  • Automatically uploading sensor data to help improve the dashboard’s accuracy.
  • Accessing personalized, hyper-local air quality data through the dashboard and on the sensor.

AI-Powered Route Optimization

Our AI optimizes cycling routes by:

  • Analyzing real-time air quality data to suggest the cleanest routes.
  • Factoring in historical data, weather patterns, traffic conditions, crowdsourced data.
  • Adapting dynamically to provide cyclists with updated routes as conditions change.

AI Insights for Public Officials

Policymakers benefit by being able to:

  • Track air quality before and after a policy intervention per neighborhood.
  • Choose neighborhoods for further transportation policy interventions based on the data.
  • Get the data communicated in easy-to-understand language for NYC residents to be aware of when casting their ballots at the coming election.

Scale & Feasibility

This solution is scalable, practical, and designed for rapid deployment:

  • High GHG Reduction Potential: Clean route recommendations encourage cycling and reduce vehicle dependence, cutting emissions.
  • Rapid Time to Market: Leveraging existing infrastructure, the MVP can be built in 6-8 months using current AI models and off-the-shelf sensors.
  • Real-World Practicality: NYC's growing cycling infrastructure and government focus on sustainability make this solution timely and feasible.

Research & Business Case

This solution is based on solid research and market demand. Cycling in NYC is steadily increasing, and air pollution poses health risks to cyclists. Additionally, city officials are focused on data-driven environmental policies, creating a market for actionable insights provided by our dashboard.

Key Challenges

  • Data Privacy: Cyclists may have concerns about sharing their data. Our platform will anonymize data and provide control over what is shared.
  • Sensor Reliability: We will use novel data assimilation to make strong use of sensors even without great data quality

Revenue Model

We will generate revenue through sensor sales, subscription-based premium features, and licensing our enriched dataset to third parties like urban planners and health organizations.
